Facilitating the Efficient Recruitment of Grad Students in a CRM

Introduction

I worked on this project during my internship at All Campus, a higher-ed marketing company. Archa is a CRM (customer relationship management) software. The prospect table feature in Archa help enrollment advisors reach out to leads (prospective students) and recruit them for graduate programs. The table lists the prospective students of different programs and their personal information. Enrollment advisors use the call or email feature to contact leads and record their information and status in the table.

Problem

I was tasked with working with another designer to redesign the page following a new design system and improve its usability.

We conducted user research through interviewing 2 enrollment advisors, observing an advisor reach out to 20+ prospects, and analyzing survey responses from 21 advisors, some of whom used a competitor software. Our research uncovered the following issues, which we addressed in the redesign. ➝

  1. The way the “priority rating” ranks the leads doesn’t take time zones into account, so some leads cannot be called even though they’re “high priority.”
  2. Currently the history of communication between the advisor and a lead can only be accessed by opening a modal. How might we give advisors a summary of their past interactions in a way that’s more easily accessible?
  3. The UI of the prospect table is cluttered, unaligned, and confusing. How might we represent different functionalities more clearly in the UI?

1. Helping enrollment advisors determine which leads to call

The table displays the priority column as a number of stars.

The Archa platform automatically assigns a priority rating to the leads in the system, and ideally all an advisor needs to do is go down the list and dial each number. However, we found through user interviews that prospects ranked as high priority were sometimes in time zones that made them unreachable (regulations dictate that prospects may only be called 8am-8pm). Hence, advisors always manually check the prospect’s information before dialing.

How might we communicate the availability of the leads to enrollment advisors?

To make it easier for advisors to avoid calling prospects at inappropriate times, I explored different ways of making it obvious when a prospect is un-callable.
