About

“What do you want to do when you grow up?” was a question I had trouble answering. In many people’s eyes, my love of art and literature contradicted my knack for math and computer science.

In my first software engineering internship last summer, I happened to sit in on a UX design working session, and I found a place where these disparate parts of my life came together: the visual thinking acquired from years of designing graphics and making art, the empathy accumulated from reading broadly, and the front-end technical skills that help me understand the implementation of UI elements.

Since deciding to pursue the field of UX, I have grown through internships and personal projects that expanded my understanding of the nuances of product design problems. After I graduate, I hope to keep growing in a work environment in which I tackle interesting problems in a collaborative environment.

Calling Home: A Sonnet

I video call my mother late at night
(roughly at noon on her side of the Earth)
to tell a story or some joke that might
provoke her ringing laugh. This sound of mirth
evokes late evenings, walking by her side
in the cool breeze, after a warm day.
Sometimes the boundless water that divides
us doesn’t really feel like such a long way.
Her voice breaks. “Would you mind repeating that?”
I asked her when the crackling fades.
And when I try to joke about our cat,
her laugh arrives, just a moment late.
“No battery left!” And then the screen went black.
I wish her face would somehow flicker back.
